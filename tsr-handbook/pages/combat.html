Flow of Combat

Before Combat

All participants roll their Dexterity (+ modifiers) for initiative. The highest initiative acts first. Ties are broken by the higher Dexterity stat die.

Before the first round, all combatants may take one Free Interact Action (draw up to 2 pieces of equipment) and one Pre-Battle Action (self-buff, terrain spell, potion, or guard stance — nothing that targets, damages, or moves enemies).

The Player Turn

Each turn has an Upkeep Phase followed by your 3 Actions.

Upkeep Phase
Pay costs for active self-buffs, roll to Break Free from persistent CCs, count down duration effects, and roll DoT damage (unless you Cleanse on your first action).
3 Actions
Spend your 3 Actions in any combination: Attack, Cast, Move, Interact, etc. Free Actions do not cost anything and can even be used outside your turn.

The Enemy Phase

When enemies act, you have 1 Reaction to defend yourself: Defensive Actions (dodge/block), Counter Attacks (Guard Stance required), or Reactive Spells.

Reactions (Enemy Phase)

You have 1 Reaction per enemy phase. If you successfully Dodge all attacks in a phase (or Block taking 0 HP damage on the first hit), your Reaction is refunded.

Attack of Opportunity: Triggered when an enemy moves through your melee reach, stands from Prone, makes a Ranged Attack, casts a Spell, or Interacts with an object. Requires CQC Adept, Relentless, or Overwatch. Taking AoO damage interrupts spellcasting and object interactions.

Attacking

Roll your Accuracy die (Dex for physical, Mind for spells) + modifiers versus the target's Parry. If your roll ≥ Parry, the attack hits. Then check your damage against their Armor or Ward.

Multiple Attack Penalty (MAP)

Each attack after the first in your player phase incurs a penalty:

  • 1st attack — no penalty
  • 2nd attack — −5 Accuracy
  • 3rd attack — −10 Accuracy

MAP does not apply to Attacks of Opportunity or Counter Attacks.

Critical Hits

When your Accuracy roll exceeds the target's Parry by 4 or more, you score a Critical Hit. Roll one extra weapon die and add it to damage. Spells cannot crit unless stated otherwise.

Offensive Martial Stances

Dual Strike — 2 Actions
Attack twice at current MAP (MAP only increases after both strikes). Dual Wielding required. −2 Accuracy if either weapon lacks Finesse.

Strike once, add Strength twice to damage. Two-Handed Melee only. Counts as 2 attacks for MAP.

Casting Magic

Spells cost 2 Actions by default (self-buffs: 1 Action). You need at least one free hand to cast — unless you've learned Touch Magic or a hands-free technique.

Spell RankMana CostSlot Cost
Novice2 Mana2 Slots
Adept4 Mana3 Slots
Master8 Mana4 Slots
Casting with one free hand grants −1 Mana cost to all spells. Self-buff spells go into your Concentration Slot — only one magical self-buff at a time.

Spell Range

Base spell range = 2. Each point of Mind and each point of Willpower both add +1 to your spell range.

Touch Magic

Advanced battlemage technique. Pour a spell through a weapon attack (2 Actions total), using either weapon accuracy or spell accuracy as the delivery. Requires specialized training. Only works on single-target spells with a casting cost of 2 or fewer actions.

Summoning

Summons require 1 Action per turn to command, granting the summon 2 Actions. Swarm Summons follow group orders only. Effective command range: full visible battlefield (~500ft). Summons take your Concentration slot.

Damage & Resistances

Armor & Ward

  • Armor (Physical Resistance) = Half Vigor − 2 + equipment bonuses
  • Ward (Magical Resistance) = Half Willpower − 2 + equipment bonuses

If damage exceeds the matching resistance, only the excess deals HP damage.

Damage Shields

A secondary HP pool that absorbs all incoming damage until broken. Damage Shields do not cause Downstate when depleted. Cap = 5 at d4 Vigor, +5 per upgrade. DoTs bypass Damage Shields (except Acid, which targets them first).

Armor Penetration (AP)

AP ignores physical resistance points up to its value, and adds that value directly to damage against Damage Shields. Cannot reduce resistance below 0. Does not work against resistance gained from blocking.

Primary vs Secondary Damage

  • Primary Damage: the main physical hit (or spell hit); boosted by Primary Damage stat.
  • Secondary Damage: enchantments, Touch Magic, resins carried through the Primary. Max 3 sources. One main source gets full modifiers; extras get halved.
  • Spell Power: boosts spell damage and Mind-based attacks.

Effective Damage

When a weapon or spell type matches a target's weakness, all attack dice are maximized. Crit extra dice are also maximized; Deadly crits also maximize the Stat die.

Crowd Control Effects

CC is split into Physical CC (resisted by Vigor) and Magical CC (resisted by Willpower). Applying CC uses an opposed roll — aggressor's relevant stat vs. defender's resistance stat. Equal = defender wins.

Once applied, targets may Break Free at the start of their turn, rolling their resistance vs. a target of 2 + Half Attacker's Stat. Each failed attempt adds a cumulative +2 bonus to subsequent attempts.

Damage Over Time

DoT damage bypasses all Armor and Ward. The only way to stop it is Cleansing (First Aid or relevant abilities) used as your first action in the DoT Phase. Stacks are consumed in a half-life pattern: half of total stacks trigger per tick (round up).

Recovery & Resting

Long Rest (≈6 hours)
Full recovery of HP, Stamina, Mana, and all Wounds. All status effects cleared (unless stated otherwise).
Short Rest
50% recovery of max attributes. Each party member may recover 1 Wound. Only specifically stated injuries/status effects are cleared.
Wounds
Each time a character is Downed, they gain a Wound (−5 Max HP, min 5 HP). Wounds persist until a Short or Long Rest.
Downstate
At 0 HP: cannot act, all self-buffs/augments drop, gain 1 Wound. If HP drops to negative equal to current Max HP — Unconscious (out of combat). Heal to ≥1 HP to rejoin the fight.

Flanking: When you and at least 1 ally are on opposite sides of an enemy (within 5ft), that enemy is flanked. +1 Accuracy per flanking pair (max +4 with 4 pairs).

Flying

Flying units ignore terrain effects (positive and negative), but suffer weakness to ranged physical and wind damage. Landing or taking flight costs 1 Free Action per turn, and hovering incurs a −3 penalty to ranged attack range. Taking primary effective damage while hovering causes a fall (50 spaces/turn until a Hard Dex check succeeds).

Falling Damage

Falling 1 space (5ft) = no damage. Each space fallen beyond 10ft deals 1d8 + X (X = number of spaces above 10) physical damage.
