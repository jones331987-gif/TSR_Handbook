Attributes

Attributes are your core resource pools used to cast spells, perform techniques, and survive combat. All characters begin with the same base and spend additional Attribute Points to customize their build.

Health (HP)
Determines how much damage you can take before going Downed. At 0 HP you cannot act and suffer a Wound (−5 Max HP until rested).
Stamina
Fuels physical Techniques, dodges, blocks, and attack enhancers. At 0 Stamina you lose 1 Action per turn until you recover at least 1 point.
Mana
Required to cast Spells and fuel magical abilities. At 0 Mana you suffer Manashock, removing magical self-buffs and dispelling active summons.

Stats

Stats govern your combat effectiveness across five pillars. Every character starts with a d4 in each stat, and 6 Stat Points to increase them. Each point raises the die by one step.

d4 → d6 → d8 → d10 → d12 → d12+1 → … → d12+5
Strength
Physical weapon damage. Required to wield heavy weapons. Used to apply physical crowd control (vs. opponent's Vigor).
Dexterity
Accuracy for attacks. Determines Parry = 2 + Half Dex. Higher Dex = harder to hit you.
Vigor
Durability and Armor = Half Vigor − 2. Each upgrade adds +5 to HP and Damage Shield cap. Resists physical CC.
Mind
Spell accuracy and spell damage. Also increases Spell Range by +1 per point (base 2).
Willpower
Ward = Half Willpower − 2. Resists magical CC. Also increases Spell Range +1 per point (base 2).

Skills Overview

Skills are your character's specializations — think RPG skill trees. You begin with 3 Skill Points. Each point raises a skill one rank: Novice (1), Adept (2), Master (3). Higher ranks unlock better Spells and Techniques.

Technique Slots = your max Stamina. Spell Slots = your max Mana. Rank costs: Novice = 2 slots · Adept = 3 slots · Master = 4 slots.

Combat Roles

Your Role signals what you're built to do on the battlefield, and determines which Trial you must pass at Tier thresholds. Choose a role that matches your planned Skills and playstyle.

⚔ Warrior
Frontline tank. Uses Guard effects to redirect attacks aimed at allies. Built around HP and Armor. Essential for protecting the team.
🗡 Assassin
Single-target threat elimination. High Accuracy and Movement. Prioritizes dangers and strikes before falling back.
💥 Destroyer
Area damage powerhouse. Clears weak enemies while damaging untouched ones and weakening clusters for follow-up.
✶ Support
Healing, buffing, and purging negative effects. Monitors ally HP and conditions throughout every fight.
⛓ Disabler
Crowd control specialist. Strips Poise, roots enemies, and creates openings for assassins and destroyers.

Character Sheet

TSR character sheets have two parts: the Social Sheet (personality, appearance, history) and the Combat Sheet (all mechanical values). Both are required for character submission.

Combat Sheet — Required Fields

  • Name — Used for Transaction posts when buying Spells, Techniques, and Equipment.
  • Level — Starts at 1.
  • Role — Your combat function (see above).
  • Starting Boon — Your chosen Boon from the Boons list.
  • Attributes — HP / Stamina / Mana after spending your 20 Attribute Points.
  • Stats — Str / Dex / Vig / Min / Wil after spending your 6 Stat Points.
  • Skills — Which skills you've invested your 3 Skill Points into.
  • Equipment — Weapons, armor, and other purchased gear.
  • Money — Remaining Silver after purchases (start with 500).
  • Techniques & Spells — Readied (within slot limit) and Learned.
  • Reference Section — Parry, Armor, Ward, Movement, and attacks pre-calculated.

Leveling Up

Characters earn 10 Experience to level up. Experience is split among the party after each combat. Fighting enemies 1 Tier below you grants only half XP; 2 Tiers below grants none.

Step-by-Step Creation

Step 1 — Start with Your Vision
Write down what you already know: your character's name, concept, fighting style. Even just "Level 1" is a start. Having a clear archetype (mighty axe-fighter, delicate duelist, blazing mage) speeds everything else up.
Step 2 — Choose Skills & Role
Pick your Role and spend your 3 Skill Points. Each skill has been designed with a specific archetype in mind. CQC + Endurance suits warriors; Anima + Spellcrafting suits blasters; Figment + Martial Arts suits tricksters.
Step 3 — Set Attributes & Stats
Fighters prioritize Stamina and Str/Dex. Casters prioritize Mana and Mind/Willpower. Tanks want HP and Vigor. Spend your 6 Stat Points and 20 Attribute Points accordingly.
Step 4 — Choose Equipment, Spells & Techniques
Browse the Items List, Spells, and Techniques with your 500 Silver. You can only ready Techniques up to your max Stamina in slots, and Spells up to your max Mana. Plan your loadout carefully.
Step 5 — Pick Your Boon & Run the Numbers
Choose a Starting Boon from the Boons page, then calculate: Parry (2+Half Dex), Armor (Half Vig−2), Ward (Half Wil−2), Accuracy (Dex die), Damage (Str + weapon die). Revise if needed, then submit for review.
